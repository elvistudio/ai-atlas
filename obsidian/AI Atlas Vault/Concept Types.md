# Concept Types

#concept-types #typed-map #ai-atlas

AI Atlas is not only a hierarchy. It is a typed knowledge map.

Each node has two different properties:

1. Hierarchy level — where it sits in the tree.
2. Concept type — what kind of thing it is.

## Why this matters

Some AI concepts are fields.
Some are major areas.
Some are subfields.
Some are paradigms.
Some are methods.
Some are architectures.
Some are model families.
Some are techniques.
Some are components.
Some are systems or products.
Some are cross-cutting concerns.

A clean AI map should not force all concepts to behave as if they are the same kind of entity.

## Concept type list

### Field

The root field.

Example:

- [[00 Artificial Intelligence|Artificial Intelligence]]

### Major Area

A large, relatively stable area of AI.

Examples:

- [[Level 1/Machine Learning|Machine Learning]]
- [[Level 1/Symbolic AI|Symbolic AI]]
- [[Level 1/Robotics and Embodied AI|Robotics and Embodied AI]]

### Cross-Cutting Area

An area that cuts across multiple branches of AI.

Examples:

- [[Level 1/Generative AI|Generative AI]]
- [[Level 1/AI Safety, Alignment and Governance|AI Safety, Alignment and Governance]]

### Future Area

A future-facing or speculative area of AI.

- [[Level 1/AGI and Future AI|AGI and Future AI]]

### Subfield

A major subdivision inside a larger AI area.

Examples:

### Paradigm

A broad way of approaching learning, reasoning, or system design.

Examples:

- [[Level 2/Supervised Learning|Supervised Learning]]

A specific approach or family of approaches.

Future examples:

### Architecture

A structural design for models or systems.

### Model Family

A family of models with shared purpose or architecture.

Examples:

- [[Level 2/Large Language Models|Large Language Models]]

### Technique

### Component

- Re-ranker

### System Pattern

- AI Agent
- RAG Pipeline
