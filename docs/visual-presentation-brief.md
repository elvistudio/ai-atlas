# Visual Presentation Brief

This brief defines how the current AI Atlas taxonomy should be explored as a static visual artifact for print or printable PDF.

The current project status is Level 1 and Level 2 only. This brief should not add Level 3 concepts or change taxonomy content.

## Goals

A static AI Atlas map should:

- make the field understandable at a glance,
- show the current Level 1 and Level 2 taxonomy clearly,
- remain readable when printed,
- preserve the distinction between hierarchy level and concept type,
- support review and critique,
- avoid visual overload.

## Current scope

The first printable prototypes should include:

- Level 0 — Artificial Intelligence,
- Level 1 — major areas and cross-cutting areas,
- Level 2 — main subareas.

Level 3 and deeper concepts should be excluded unless the prototype is explicitly marked as a future draft.

## Candidate formats

### Essentials Poster

Audience: general readers, students, and introductory presentations.

Recommended size: A3 or A2.

Content: Level 0, all Level 1 areas, and selected or summarized Level 2 concepts.

Purpose: explain the shape of modern AI quickly without overwhelming first-time readers.

### Full L1-L2 Poster

Audience: technical readers, reviewers, educators, and AI practitioners.

Recommended size: A1 or A0.

Content: Level 0, all Level 1 areas, all Level 2 main subareas, and a compact legend.

Purpose: present the complete current AI Atlas v0.1 taxonomy for discussion and critique.

### Review Wall Map

Audience: expert reviewers, contributors, and workshop participants.

Recommended size: A0 or larger PDF / wall print.

Content: all current Level 1 and Level 2 concepts, optional numbering, room for comments, and visible review prompts.

Purpose: make the taxonomy easy to mark up and review.

## Candidate layouts

### Radial atlas

Level 0 sits in the center, Level 1 areas form the main ring, and Level 2 concepts are grouped around each Level 1 parent.

Strengths: memorable, map-like, good for visual identity.

Risks: long labels and dense branches can become hard to place.

### Modular grid

Each Level 1 area gets its own panel, with Level 2 concepts listed inside the panel.

Strengths: highly readable, easy to print, good for complete Level 1-Level 2 coverage.

Risks: less visually map-like, weaker at showing cross-cutting relationships.

### Layered tree

Level 0 leads to Level 1 branches, with Level 2 concepts under each branch.

Strengths: simple and structurally clear.

Risks: can become too wide or too tall and may look like an outline rather than a designed map.

### Subway-map style

Major branches act like lines and Level 2 concepts act like stations.

Strengths: memorable and navigable.

Risks: may imply sequence or routes where none exist.

## Recommended first prototype

The first static prototype should be a Full L1-L2 Poster using a modular grid layout.

The modular grid is the safest first prototype because it maximizes readability and reviewability.

A radial atlas should be tested next as a stronger visual identity candidate after the content density is understood.

## Visual hierarchy

Use hierarchy level to control:

- section size,
- typographic weight,
- spacing,
- grouping.

Use concept type to control:

- small labels,
- badges,
- icons,
- legend categories.

Do not rely only on color to encode essential meaning.

## Print readability requirements

- Level 1 labels must be readable at a distance.
- Level 2 labels must be readable when standing close to the poster.
- Avoid text that becomes too small at the intended print size.
- Prefer whitespace over decorative density.
- Keep the legend short and close to the map.
- Avoid many cross-links in the first print version.

## Review questions

Before accepting a static visual format, ask:

- Can a new reader understand the overall field quickly?
- Can a technical reviewer find a specific Level 2 concept quickly?
- Is hierarchy level visually distinct from concept type?
- Does the design create false relationships?
- Is the map printable without relying on zoom or interaction?
