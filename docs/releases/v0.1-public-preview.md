# AI Atlas v0.1 Public Preview

Status: public preview release notes  
Release scope: Level 0, Level 1, and Level 2 taxonomy  
Canonical status: Level 1 and Level 2 only  
Level 3 status: draft/review work only, not canonical

AI Atlas v0.1 is the first public preview of the stable top layers of the AI Atlas taxonomy.

This release is intended for public review and discussion. It is not a final, authoritative, exhaustive, or complete taxonomy of Artificial Intelligence.

## Recommended public announcement

```text
AI Atlas v0.1 is open for public review.

This first public preview covers the stable top layers of the map: Level 1 major areas and Level 2 main subareas of modern Artificial Intelligence.

We are looking for feedback on missing major AI areas, misplaced or overlapping Level 1 branches, missing important Level 2 subareas, unclear names, and hierarchy usefulness for learning and discussion.

Level 3 is being developed separately as draft/review work and is not canonical yet.
```

## What is included

This public preview includes:

- Level 0 — Artificial Intelligence,
- Level 1 — major AI areas,
- Level 2 — main subareas,
- public taxonomy documentation,
- public generated taxonomy views aligned with the current Level 1–Level 2 scope,
- a public review guide,
- release-readiness documentation.

Canonical taxonomy files:

- `taxonomy/ai-taxonomy-l1-l2.json`
- `taxonomy/ai-taxonomy-l1-l2.md`

Review guide:

- `PUBLIC_REVIEW.md`

Release-readiness documents:

- `docs/public-preview-readiness.md`
- `docs/public-preview-readiness-review.md`

## What is not included

This public preview does not include:

- canonical Level 3 taxonomy,
- full Level 3 branch coverage,
- relation graph implementation,
- public website and search,
- final poster or commercial visual product,
- Level 4 or Level 5 expansion,
- products, company-specific systems, or concrete model versions as canonical taxonomy nodes.

## Review focus

Reviewers should focus on:

1. missing Level 1 areas,
2. Level 1 areas that may be too broad, too narrow, overlapping, or misplaced,
3. missing important Level 2 subareas,
4. Level 2 concepts that may be under the wrong parent,
5. unclear or unstable names,
6. whether the hierarchy is useful for learning, discussion, and future expansion.

Reviewers should not treat Level 3 drafts as canonical.

## Release guardrails

Use cautious release language:

- `public preview`,
- `v0.1`,
- `open for review`,
- `Level 1 and Level 2`,
- `draft/review work` for Level 3.

Avoid overclaiming:

- do not call the taxonomy complete,
- do not call it final,
- do not call it authoritative,
- do not imply Level 3 is canonical,
- do not imply the full knowledge graph or public website is ready,
- do not imply poster/commercial product launch.

## Release decision

AI Atlas v0.1 may be shown publicly as a scoped public preview of the Level 1 and Level 2 taxonomy.

The public preview is ready because:

- current scope is clearly stated,
- canonical source files are identified,
- Level 3 boundaries are explicit,
- public/private separation is documented,
- public review guidance exists,
- release-readiness criteria and review notes exist,
- no must-fix public-preview blockers were found in the documentation scope.

## Suggested next work after release

After the public preview is published, the next work should be:

1. collect and triage public feedback on Level 1 and Level 2,
2. resolve naming and hierarchy issues before deeper expansion,
3. formalize canonical JSON/schema validation,
4. keep Markdown and generated views synchronized with canonical JSON,
5. continue Level 3 as draft/review work before any canonical promotion.
