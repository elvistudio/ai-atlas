Attribution

How to attribute AI Atlas

Keep attribution close to the reused material, especially on slides, posters, handouts, screenshots, and adapted diagrams.

Short attribution

Based on AI Atlas v0.1 - ai-atlas-project/ai-atlas

Longer attribution

Based on AI Atlas v0.1 public preview, an open taxonomy and knowledge map of modern Artificial Intelligence. Canonical source: ai-atlas-project/ai-atlas.

Adapted-material attribution

Adapted from AI Atlas v0.1 public preview. This adaptation is not an official AI Atlas release.

Source status

Official, draft, and adapted materials

Official public AI Atlas material

Materials intentionally published by AI Atlas for public reuse.

  • Include the AI Atlas version.
  • Include the source repository.
  • Include a publication or generation date when available.
  • Include license or reuse terms when available.

Private or draft material

Internal planning, experiments, design drafts, teacher-pack drafts, unreleased posters, or private review materials.

  • Do not cite private drafts as public AI Atlas releases.
  • Do not mix private draft content into public assets without review and promotion.

Third-party or adapted material

Materials created by users, teachers, writers, or organizations based on AI Atlas.

  • Label them as adapted.
  • Include the AI Atlas version and public source.
  • Do not imply official endorsement or approval.
